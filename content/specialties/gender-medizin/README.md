# Gender-Medizin — Flagship Focus Area

**Wikimedica Specialty Area: Gender-Medizin**
**Status: Active — Editorial Flagship Priority**

---

## 1. Overview

Gender-Medizin (sex- and gender-sensitive medicine) is one of the most rapidly developing fields in contemporary medicine. It addresses the systematic differences in disease presentation, pathophysiology, pharmacology, and treatment outcomes between biological sexes (male, female, intersex) and across gender identities.

Wikimedica designates Gender-Medizin as its **editorial flagship focus area**, meaning:

- It receives **expanded PubMed surveillance** (daily, with a broader search term set)
- It has priority access to **reviewer and editor capacity**
- Cross-specialty articles are evaluated for gender-relevant content by the Gender-Medizin editor
- AT Medical actively recruits qualified Gender-Medizin specialists as authors and reviewers

This reflects the recognition that sex and gender differences remain systematically underrepresented in mainstream medical education resources — a gap that Wikimedica is committed to addressing.

## 2. Scope

Gender-Medizin content on Wikimedica covers:

### 2.1 Sex-Specific Disease Manifestations

- Cardiovascular: atypical MI presentation in women, sex differences in heart failure phenotypes
- Neurological: sex differences in stroke risk factors, MS incidence, and migraine
- Pharmacological: sex-specific drug metabolism, dosing implications, adverse event profiles
- Immunological: sex differences in autoimmune disease prevalence and severity
- Oncological: sex differences in cancer incidence, staging, and treatment response

### 2.2 Gender and Health

- Impact of gender identity on healthcare access, communication, and outcomes
- Mental health across gender identities
- Gender-sensitive communication and consultation techniques
- Non-binary and transgender health (endocrinology, surgery, psychiatry)

### 2.3 Research Methodology

- Historical exclusion of women from clinical trials — consequences and current standards
- ICH E11A and CONSORT sex/gender reporting requirements
- Interpretation of sex-disaggregated trial data
- Intersectionality in medical research

### 2.4 Education and Clinical Practice

- Gender-sensitive history-taking
- Bias in pain assessment and treatment
- Gender dimensions in end-of-life care
- Gender-sensitive patient education

## 3. Editorial Priorities

The Gender-Medizin editorial team focuses first on topics where the evidence gap or practice gap is greatest:

1. **Herzinfarkt bei Frauen** — atypical presentation, diagnostic delay, outcomes disparity
2. **Geschlechtsspezifische Pharmakologie** — sex differences in drug metabolism (CYP isoenzymes), QT prolongation, dosing
3. **Chronischer Schmerz und Gender** — pain bias in clinical settings, fibromyalgia, endometriosis underdiagnosis
4. **Autoimmunerkrankungen und Geschlecht** — SLE, MS, Hashimoto
5. **Depression: Geschlechtsunterschiede** — diagnostic criteria, presentation, treatment response
6. **Gesundheit trans* und nicht-binärer Personen** — evidence-based care, clinical communication
7. **Geschlechtsspezifische Aspekte in der Onkologie** — lung cancer in female non-smokers, sex differences in immunotherapy response
8. **Kardiometabolisches Risiko bei Frauen** — PCOS, menopause, gestational diabetes as CV risk factors

### Condition-Specific Terms

```
("myocardial infarction"[MeSH] OR "acute coronary syndrome"[MeSH]) AND ("sex factors"[MeSH] OR "women"[MeSH])
("pharmacokinetics"[MeSH] OR "drug metabolism"[MeSH]) AND "sex factors"[MeSH]
("pain"[MeSH] OR "chronic pain"[MeSH]) AND "sex factors"[MeSH]
("autoimmune diseases"[MeSH]) AND "sex factors"[MeSH]
("clinical trials as topic"[MeSH]) AND ("sex factors"[MeSH] OR "women"[MeSH] OR "sex disaggregated"[tw])
```

### Filter

All Gender-Medizin searches include human studies only: `AND "humans"[MeSH]`

## 6. Author and Reviewer Requirements

### Specialty Qualification for Gender-Medizin

Given the interdisciplinary nature of Gender-Medizin, reviewers and authors may qualify through:

- Board certification in any clinical specialty **plus** documented experience in gender-sensitive medicine (publications, teaching, or clinical programme involvement), **or**
- Postgraduate training specifically in Gender-Medizin or Women's Health, **or**
- Research background with peer-reviewed publications in the field.

### Gender-Medizin Editor

The designated Gender-Medizin editor:
- Coordinates PubMed alert responses for the specialty.
- Assigns authors and reviewers for planned articles.
- Performs cross-specialty gender-relevance tagging for articles in other specialties.
- Reports to the Medical Director and Editorial Board.
- Is ideally board-certified in a clinical specialty with an active Gender-Medizin research or clinical programme role.
